Contact Guidelines

When Reaching Out

To ensure effective communication and respect for Dinah's time and community commitments, please consider the following when making contact:

  • Be Specific: Clearly state the purpose of your inquiry and what you're hoping to achieve
  • Provide Context: Include information about your organization, event, or project
  • Timeline: Indicate your timeline and any deadlines
  • Compensation: For speaking engagements, please discuss honorarium and travel arrangements
  • Accessibility: Mention any accessibility needs or accommodations

Community-Centered Approach

Dinah's work is grounded in community accountability and intersectional principles. When reaching out, please consider:

  • Community Benefit: How will this collaboration benefit marginalized communities?
  • Intersectional Approach: Does your project/event center intersectional analysis?
  • Community Representation: Are marginalized voices centered in your work?
  • Long-term Impact: What are the sustainable outcomes of this collaboration?
  • Resource Sharing: How are resources and power shared in this collaboration?

Response and Availability

Due to the high volume of requests and Dinah's extensive commitments to community work and organizational leadership, please allow adequate time for responses to inquiries.

Priority Areas

Requests in the following areas may receive priority consideration:

  • Community-led initiatives and organizations
  • BPOC-led projects and events
  • Intersectional activism and organizing
  • Policy work that centers marginalized voices
  • Educational initiatives in academic or community settings
  • International collaboration on human rights

Scheduling Considerations

Please note that Dinah's availability may be limited due to:

  • Ongoing organizational leadership responsibilities
  • Community commitments and mutual aid work
  • International travel for advocacy and policy work
  • Personal health and community care priorities

Early booking for speaking engagements and collaborations is recommended.
